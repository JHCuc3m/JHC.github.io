Jiahao Chen

M.S. in Computational Science and Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology

I'm a Computational Science and Engineering graduate student at Georgia Tech. My passion lies at the intersection of high-performance computing and machine learning, and I'm driven by the challenge of optimizing complex systems—whether it's an SMT logic solver or an ML compiler.

My research focuses on applying techniques like Bayesian optimization and reinforcement learning to tune SMT solvers (like CVC5) and analyzing the performance of ML compilers such as PyTorch Dynamo. Beyond research, I have a strong background in full-stack software development, with hands-on experience building systems using React, FastAPI, C#, and cloud-native tools like Docker and Google Cloud Platform.

This technical drive is balanced by a background in competitive programming. Outside of the lab, I'm a competitive ballroom dancer with the Georgia Tech team and enjoy sports like Ultimate Frisbee and basketball. I am a native speaker of Spanish and Chinese, and fluent in English.
